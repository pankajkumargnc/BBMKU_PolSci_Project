# 📚 Module 1: Master Syllabus Analysis & CBCS Framework

To score a **Gold Medal** in your M.A. and crack **UGC NET JRF**, the very first step is to master the syllabus. The syllabus is not just a list of topics; it is the boundary line of your battlefield. 

This module provides a detailed breakdown of the **BBMKU Choice Based Credit System (CBCS)** structure and maps it directly against the **UGC NET Paper II** syllabus to identify overlapping areas where you can study once and score twice.

## 1. BBMKU M.A. Political Science: CBCS Pattern & Credit Structure

The Master of Arts (M.A.) in Political Science under BBMKU follows the UGC-mandated CBCS (Choice Based Credit System). The program spans **2 Years (4 Semesters)**.

### 📊 Credit Distribution
- **Total Credits Required:** 80 Credits (20 Credits per semester)
- **Course Types:**
  1. **Foundation Course (FC) / Compulsory:** Foundational topics.
  2. **Core Course (CC):** Mandatory subjects central to the discipline.
  3. **Skill Enhancement Course (SEC):** Value-based or skill-based courses (e.g., Human Rights, Research Methodology).
  4. **Open Elective (OE):** Interdisciplinary subject chosen from another department.
  5. **Discipline Centric Elective (DCE):** Specialization papers chosen in Semester IV.
  6. **Project / Dissertation:** Independent research work in Semester IV.

### 📝 Semester-wise Paper Breakdown (Standard BBMKU Framework)

#### Semester I (20 Credits)
* **FC-101:** Elementary Political Science / Political Theory
* **CC-102:** Western Political Thought (Plato to Marx)
* **CC-103:** Indian Political Thought (Ancient & Modern)
* **CC-104:** Comparative Political Analysis

#### Semester II (20 Credits)
* **CC-205:** Theories of International Relations
* **CC-206:** Public Administration: Concepts & Theories
* **CC-207:** Political Processes and Institutions in India
* **SEC-201:** Skill Enhancement (e.g., Research Methodology / Human Rights)

#### Semester III (20 Credits)
* **CC-308:** India's Foreign Policy
* **CC-309:** State Politics in India (with special reference to Jharkhand)
* **CC-310:** Modern Political Analysis
* **OE-301:** Open Elective (Interdisciplinary)

#### Semester IV (20 Credits)
* **CC-411:** Governance and Public Policy in India
* **DCE-401:** Specialization Paper 1 (e.g., International Law / Local Self Govt.)
* **DCE-402:** Specialization Paper 2 (e.g., Peace & Conflict / Political Sociology)
* **PROJ-401:** Dissertation and Viva-Voce

## 2. UGC NET Political Science (Code 02) Syllabus

The UGC NET Paper II consists of **100 Objective Type Questions (MCQs)** worth 200 marks. There is no negative marking.

1. **Unit 1:** Political Theory (Concepts & Ideologies)
2. **Unit 2:** Political Thought (Western Thinkers)
3. **Unit 3:** Indian Political Thought (Dharamshastra to Ambedkar)
4. **Unit 4:** Comparative Political Analysis (Approaches & Regimes)
5. **Unit 5:** International Relations (Theories & Concepts)
6. **Unit 6:** India's Foreign Policy (Evolution & Bilateral Relations)
7. **Unit 7:** Political Institutions in India (Constitution, Executive, Judiciary)
8. **Unit 8:** Political Processes in India (Party System, Movements, Elections)
9. **Unit 9:** Public Administration (Theories & Principles)
10. **Unit 10:** Governance and Public Policy in India (RTI, MNREGA, e-Governance)

## 4. University Exam Pattern vs UGC NET Exam Pattern

### BBMKU Exam Pattern (Subjective + Objective)
* **Total Marks per Paper:** 100 Marks (70 External + 30 Internal)
* **Internal Assessment (30 Marks):** Written Test (20) + Assignment/Attendance (10)
* **End Semester Exam (70 Marks) Duration:** 3 Hours
  * **Group A:** 10 Compulsory MCQs (10 × 1 = 10 Marks) + 2 Very Short Answer (2 × 5 = 10 Marks)
  * **Group B:** 4 Long Answer Questions out of 8 (4 × 12.5 = 50 Marks)

> **Gold Medal Tip:** To score a Gold Medal, your Long Answers (Group B) must include:
> 1. Introduction, 2. Thinkers/Scholars' definitions, 3. Bullet points/Flowcharts, 4. Contemporary examples, 5. Conclusion.

### UGC NET Pattern (Purely Objective)
* **Paper I:** 50 Questions (General Aptitude, Teaching, Research) - 100 Marks
* **Paper II:** 100 Questions (Political Science) - 200 Marks
* **Format:** Computer Based Test (CBT), 3 Hours combined.
* **Target for JRF:** You need to target a minimum of **68% to 72%** (approx 105+ net correct answers out of 150) depending on the cut-off trends.
