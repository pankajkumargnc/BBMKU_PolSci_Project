## 1. Introduction to Modern Political Analysis (आधुनिक राजनीतिक विश्लेषण का परिचय)

**English:**
Traditional Political Science was philosophical, historical, and institutional. After World War II, largely influenced by American political scientists (the "Chicago School"), there was a massive shift towards making political science a rigorous, empirical "Science." This shift is known as Modern Political Analysis, characterized by the study of *actual human behavior* rather than formal institutions.

**Hindi (हिंदी व्याख्या):**
पारंपरिक राजनीति विज्ञान दार्शनिक, ऐतिहासिक और संस्थागत था। द्वितीय विश्व युद्ध के बाद, मुख्य रूप से अमेरिकी राजनीति विज्ञानियों ("शिकागो स्कूल") से प्रभावित होकर, राजनीति विज्ञान को एक कठोर, अनुभवजन्य (empirical) "विज्ञान" बनाने की दिशा में एक बड़ा बदलाव आया। इस बदलाव को आधुनिक राजनीतिक विश्लेषण के रूप में जाना जाता है, जिसकी विशेषता औपचारिक संस्थाओं के बजाय *वास्तविक मानवीय व्यवहार* का अध्ययन है।

## 2. Behavioralism (व्यवहारवाद)

Behavioralism emerged in the 1950s as a protest against the traditional approaches. Its primary goal was to study political behavior using scientific, quantitative methods.

### Key Proponents (प्रमुख समर्थक):
- **Charles E. Merriam:** Father of the Behavioral approach (*New Aspects of Politics, 1925*).
- **David Easton:** The intellectual godfather of the movement.
- **Robert Dahl, Harold Lasswell, Arthur Bentley.**

### David Easton’s 8 Characteristics of Behavioralism (Intellectual Foundation Stones)

David Easton summarized the core tenets of Behavioralism in 8 points (also known as the "Credo of Behavioralism"):

1. **Regularities (नियमितताएं):** Human political behavior has discoverable uniformities/regularities that can be expressed as generalizations or theories.
2. **Verification (सत्यापन):** The validity of such generalizations must be testable.
3. **Techniques (तकनीक):** Use of rigorous scientific methods (statistics, math, sampling) for data collection.
4. **Quantification (परिमाणीकरण):** Data and findings must be quantified and measured.
5. **Values (मूल्य-निरपेक्षता):** Complete separation of facts and values. Science deals with "what is", not "what ought to be". (Value-neutral research).
6. **Systematization (व्यवस्थीकरण):** Theory and research must be closely intertwined.
7. **Pure Science (विशुद्ध विज्ञान):** The goal is to understand political life (pure research) rather than immediately solving social problems (applied research).
8. **Integration (समग्रता):** Political Science must integrate with other social sciences (Sociology, Psychology, Economics) since human behavior is a whole.

## 3. Post-Behavioralism (उत्तर-व्यवहारवाद)

By the late 1960s, America was dealing with the Vietnam War, the Civil Rights Movement, and the threat of nuclear war. Behavioralists, obsessed with "pure science" and "quantification," had completely ignored these burning social issues.

In **1969**, addressing the American Political Science Association (APSA), **David Easton** himself launched a new revolution: **Post-Behavioralism**.

### The Two Slogans of Post-Behavioralism:
1. **Relevance (प्रासंगिकता):** Research must be relevant to the urgent social problems of the day. *"It is better to be vague than non-relevantly precise."*
2. **Action (कार्रवाई):** Knowledge must be put to work. Scientists have a moral obligation to engage in social reform.

```mermaid
graph LR
  A[Traditionalism] -->|Focus: Institutions & Values| B[Behavioralism]
  B -->|Focus: Science, Facts, Numbers| C[Post-Behavioralism]
  C -->|Focus: Relevance & Action, Facts + Values| D[Modern Political Science]
```

### Key Differences (तुलनात्मक अंतर)

| Aspect | Behavioralism | Post-Behavioralism |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Pure Science & Methodology | Applied Science & Social Problems |
| **Values** | Value-Neutrality (Facts only) | Value-Laden (Values are essential) |
| **Goal** | Knowledge for knowledge's sake | Knowledge for action and reform |
| **Intellectual Leader**| David Easton | David Easton (He led both movements) |

## 5. Question Bank & Model Answers

### A. Very Short Questions (2 Marks)
**Q1. Who is considered the father of the Behavioral approach in Political Science?**
*Ans:* Charles E. Merriam is considered the father of Behavioralism, following his seminal work *New Aspects of Politics* (1925).

**Q2. What were the two main battle cries of Post-Behavioralism?**
*Ans:* The two main slogans/cries of Post-Behavioralism were 'Relevance' and 'Action'.

### B. Long Analytical Questions (12.5 / 15 Marks)
**Q3. Examine David Easton's eight intellectual foundation stones of Behavioralism. Why did he later advocate for Post-Behavioralism? (BBMKU PYQ)**

**Model Answer Outline:**
1. **Introduction:** Define Behavioralism as a post-WWII movement aimed at making political science a rigorous empirical science.
2. **The 8 Foundation Stones:** Detail Easton’s 8 principles (Regularities, Verification, Techniques, Quantification, Values, Systematization, Pure Science, Integration).
3. **The Crisis (Late 1960s):** Explain how Behavioralism became lost in methodology and quantification, completely failing to address real-world crises like the Vietnam War, racial riots, and poverty.
4. **The Post-Behavioral Revolution (1969):** Explain Easton's presidential address to APSA. Define the concepts of 'Relevance' and 'Action'.
5. **Conclusion:** Summarize that Post-Behavioralism was not a rejection of scientific methods, but an integration of facts with values. It sought to use scientific methodology to actually solve human problems, striking a balance between traditionalism and behavioralism.

### C. UGC NET Specific MCQs (Paper II)

(A) Traditionalism

(B) The Credo of Behavioralism
(C) The Minnowbrook Manifesto

## 7. Phase 13 Mega Expansion: High-Yield Questions

### Top Short Questions (2-5 Marks)
**Q1. Define 'Political Socialization'.**
*Ans:* The lifelong process by which individuals learn and internalize political values, beliefs, and norms. Key agencies include family, school, and media.

**Q2. What are the three types of Political Culture?**
*Ans:* According to Almond and Verba: Parochial (no awareness/participation), Subject (awareness but no participation), and Participant (high awareness and active participation).

**Q3. What is the 'Iron Law of Oligarchy'?**
*Ans:* Coined by Robert Michels. It asserts that all complex organizations, regardless of how democratic they are at the start, will eventually develop into oligarchies (rule by an elite few).

**Q4. Explain Pareto's 'Circulation of Elites'.**
*Ans:* Vilfredo Pareto argued that elites are divided into 'Lions' (using force) and 'Foxes' (using cunning). Power constantly circulates between these two types of elites in society.

**Q5. What is the 'North-South Divide'?**
*Ans:* The socio-economic and political division between the wealthy, developed nations (Global North) and the poorer, developing nations (Global South).

### Top Long Analytical Questions (15-20 Marks)
**Q1. Critically examine the concept of Political Culture as elaborated by Gabriel Almond and Sidney Verba.**
*Outline:* Intro -> Definition of Political Culture -> The Civic Culture study (1963) -> Three ideal types (Parochial, Subject, Participant) -> The 'mixed' Civic Culture ideal for democracy -> Conclusion.

**Q2. Discuss the Elite Theory of Democracy with special reference to Mosca and Pareto.**
*Outline:* Intro -> Rejection of Marxist class struggle in favor of Elite vs Mass -> Pareto's Circulation of Elites -> Mosca's 'Ruling Class' (political class based on organizational skills) -> Michels' Iron Law -> Conclusion.

**Q3. Analyze the demand for a New International Economic Order (NIEO). How relevant is it today?**
*Outline:* Intro -> Context (1974 UNGA declaration by NAM/Developing countries) -> Core demands (fair trade, technology transfer, sovereignty over resources) -> Decline in the 1980s (Neoliberalism) -> Current relevance (WTO disparities, Climate Justice) -> Conclusion.
